Methods and Design

Methods: How would you collect the data and why? Interviews and Observational data. I choose to collect data using these methods because the topic of my choice equine therapy is a human and animal study that requires observation of the process and interviews of those involved. Describe the general methodology you choose for your study, to test your hypothesis(es). My methodology is to start off with interviewing people who are involved with equine psychotherapy. Then to conduct an observational study with local equine therapies and the sessions they hold. Explain why this method is the best for your purposes. This method is best for my purposes because the topic of my choice is human, and animal based that requires observation of the humans and animals and interviews of what is behind the sessions and observations. Participants: Who would you test and why? Describe the sample you would test and explain why you have chosen this sample. Include age, and language background and socio-economic information, if relevant to the design. Are there any participants you would exclude? Why, why not? There will be interviews with and EGALA horse specialist and onsite therapists of each equine therapy facility. I choose to interview these people because they both work together during the therapy sessions with both the horse and the adolescent. I choose to study two equine therapies near Portland, Helping Hands Horse Rescue and Equine Therapy Inc. in Newberg and Forward Stride Stables in Beaverton. I choose not to interview the adolescents as they do suffer with anxiety, trauma and depression and so putting them through the stress of an interview is not what I want to establish.

Design: What would the stimuli look like and why? The stimuli would look like talking with the people of choice at their ranches as well as the observations to make sure everyone is comfortable. Describe what kinds of manipulations/variations you would make or test for to test your hypothesis(es). I would make sure to research the people I interview and modify my questions to their style, likes, and position at the facility. Describe the factors you would vary if you were presenting a person with stimulus sentences. Explain how varying these factors would allow you to confirm or disconfirm your hypotheses. The factors that would vary would how I talk to certain interviewees. An example would be if I was speaking to and EGALA specialist I would ask them more questions about the horse’s behaviors and training to becoming a therapy horse. If I was talking with a therapist, I would ask them more questions about the adolescents and the process of emotions during the session. These factors would help me confirm or disconfirm my hypothesis by asking and getting the hands-on information about equine therapy from the source. Explain what significant differences you would need to find to confirm or disconfirm your hypothesis(es). How could your hypothesis(es) be disconfirmed by your data? I would need to make sure that what I find through interviews and observation are benefits and improvement in emotional health from the adolescents. If those benefits or improvements don’t appear then the data would be disconfirmed.

Controls: What kinds of factors would you need to control for in your study? I would need to speak with the people lined up in a timely matter as well as be able to observe the session without disturbing the adolescents or horse. I would have to do the study for a long period of time to establish that the adolescents do in fact benefit during their time at the sessions as most sessions run for a long period of time like regular human to human therapies do. Describe what types of effects would be likely to occur which would make your results appear to confirm, or to disconfirm your hypothesis(es). Describe how you can by your design rule out or control for apparent effects. There would need evidence of the adolescents benefits and improvement during their time with the equine therapy and conversation with those involved would also help support the evidence as well as some research-on-research articles of this topic. While observing it will be apparent to be as little as involved as much as possible to not affect the adolescent’s sessions sometimes the best way to get involved is to become an EGALA specialist and observe from that point of view which is a definite possibility or becoming an volunteer and helping with the sessions.

Procedure: How are you going to present the stimuli? What is the participant in the experiment going to do? What is the step-by-step protocol? We will start with interviews, getting to know the people in charge of the sessions and making sure they agree to the study is key. Also having their background information about the study will help when doing the observational part of the study. Then comes the observational study, depending on how the people in charge want things to go there are a few ways of seeing to the observations. One is to become a volunteer or even EGALA specialist that way you are not disturbing the session and or even helping the people working in the session. Another way is just sitting in on sessions however the adolescents would have to agree to the study and could make it difficult on them being watched.

Analysis: How will you analyze the results? What kind of results would confirm your hypothesis? What kind of results would disconfirm your hypothesis? The analyzation of the results will be first done with the interviews by coding and picking out the important aspects of each interview. For the observations with will be analyzing the notes taken during the observation and looking for the benefits and improvements as well as how the session work and what each facility dose to help their clients. Benefits and improvements would confirm my results seeing the opposite would disconfirm hypotheses.
